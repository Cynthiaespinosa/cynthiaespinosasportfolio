Cynthia Espinosa's Portfolio

Design Student



Hello! My name is Cynthia Espinosa

I am a Queens resident, and a current Design student at Queens College.
I attended The Williamsburg Highschool for Architecture & Design as a teen,
and graduated in 2014. Though felt I wanted more broader ways
to express my creativy in design rather than architecture.
So I became a freshmen at LaGuardia Community College,
and there I completed my curriculum as a Fine Arts major.
I recieved my associates degree in December of 2019.
My future goal is to pursue a career focused on animation and illustration.

I had taken classes in graphic design prior,
some digital animation, tradtional art and the like.
My strong suit is drawing, although, in this portfolio I display both
my strengths and weaknesses in design.

This website was made as a project of the ARTS 214 Web Design course,
and to showcase the projects I had completed in my time as a student of
ARTS 241 Design course.

Making and Breaking the Grid


For this first project, we were told to use Adobe Indesign; choose a magazine of our choice and recreate it in a way that improves the layout. I chose to take sections of small articles, and interviews I found of one of the popular woman directors of Hollywood, Greta Garbo. I intricately stitched together the paragraphs so that they made sense, placed the photos in a way that resembled journalist interview sections in celebrity magazines, and used Bodini Font as my typeface.


Type Anatomy


The second project deals more with typography. We were to highlight the typographic anatomy of the word we were given, and stylize it in a way that encompasses that word. I chose the word, "Worried". And for my Font, I chose one called, Brandon Grotesque Black. I stylized my type in way thats shaky, with a rippling effect shadowing the letters.


GIFS


Project 3 was based on emotional expressions using one color, and no additional elements that would than be converted into gifs. I got the emotion, "Silly", combined with the color, Cameo Pink, that's HEX code #EFBBCC. For this, I tried my best conveying anything silly, funny or weird, using the most abstract illustrations. I used different shades and tints of Cameo Pink for each one.


4 Elements.


Regarding project 4, we were to use either Photoshop, Illustrator or Indesign and create four posters in black and white, using one typeface. Each poster was to represent the elements; Earth, Air, Fire and Water. I chose Photoshop as my go to, and decided for darker backgrounds so that my designs would pop out more. I chose the font, Proxima Nova, and played around mainly with the filters of photoshop, adding many blurs, effects and Lighting renders to match the nature of each element.


FreeForm


For Project 5, we were to create a series of execution of our choice while also incorporating a color that will stand out. I got the color Queen Pink, that is HEX code #E8CCD7. I had originally chosen to go with the marketing of a Cafe. But that color was far to preppy for me to use for warm tones in cafes. So, I decided I'd incorporate cats into the mix, and make it into a cat cave. In part, because I love cats, and because I love coffee. So I combined the two and designed a cute and chic logo that fits the aesthetic of a pastel cat cafe.
